Why we exist

A generation skipped—not forgotten

For decades, "getting into tech" meant one thing: sit at a desk, open a desktop, install the tools, hope the power stays on. Universities, bootcamps, and corporate training all assumed the same hardware. Millions of young people across Africa and the Global South heard the same quiet message: this world isn't built for you yet.

Meanwhile, the most powerful computer many of them will ever own was already in their hands.

Smartphones crossed a threshold. Linux terminals on Android. Cloud CLIs from a bus seat. Git commits between classes. The same device that carried their music and messages could carry their craft—if someone would teach them how.

That is the Mobile-First Renaissance: not a downgrade from "real" computing, but a reclamation. The desk was never the requirement. Access was. And access has finally arrived.

The assumption

Tech education requires a PC, stable electricity, and infrastructure many communities never received.

The gap

Global youth left behind while the industry celebrated "mobile-first" products—never mobile-first learners.
